Brief Description:

Broad Street Ministry (BSM) is a broad-minded Christian community that practices hospitality for the homeless and works for a more just world through civic engagement. Broad Street Ministry was established in May of 2005 as a Pennsylvania 501(c)(3) not-for-profit corporation.

Challenges:

At the heart of BSM they are a Christian faith-based organization, however, their doors are open to the public for anyone who wishes to seek support. That being in mind, I had to create a brand which appealed universally to the masses. Secondly, BSM is a large church that has six different sectors working towards a common goal and the board's secondary focus was to better identify these areas but wasn’t sure how.

Solutions:

When developing BSM’s new identity I chose to go with a mosaic pattern. Mosaics are neutral, iconic & globally used in many religions. When the time came to identify the various sectors at BSM it arose naturally within the process. In Philadelphia, BSM’s church is a beautiful landmark which I was able to capture in their new logo. Then, using their new logo as the base, it was then color-coded for the different sectors.

Brief Description:

The National Association of Latino arts and CultureIs (NALAC). NALAC is exclusively dedicated to the promotion, advancement, development, and cultivation of the Latino arts field. 501(c)(3) not-for-profit corporation.

Challenges:

Latinos are a bright & vibrant culture, comprised of an abundance of color and energy. Portraying the same energy was pivotal in the development of NALAC’s rebranding. I needed to create an appearance and feel that was modern, sleek and most importantly fused seamlessly among Latin culture.

Solutions:

In Latin culture, women wear beautiful multi-colored dresses which is where NALAC’s distinct color scheme originated from. When developing NALAC’s trademark appearance, I adopted their original triangular pattern from their former website. Previously, the triangular pattern was subtle & hardly visible, after fusing the pattern & the new color scheme it became the forefront of their identity.
